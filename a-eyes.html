FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is A-Eyes?

A-Eyes is a desktop screen capture tool built for developers and AI power users. It lets you record your screen, narrate what you're doing with your mic, and send the capture directly to your AI coding assistant for analysis. Think of it as the missing bridge between what's on your screen and what your AI can see.

Does A-Eyes work with Claude Code?

Yes. A-Eyes integrates directly with Claude Code's CLI. It builds an optimized prompt with your screen capture, transcript, and notes, then launches it in a terminal window. Your capture lands right in an active Claude Code session.

Does A-Eyes work with Codex Desktop?

Yes. A-Eyes can detect an open Codex Desktop window and paste your capture prompt directly into it. If Codex isn't running, the prompt is copied to your clipboard so you can paste it manually.

Can I use A-Eyes to record bugs for an AI coding assistant?

Absolutely. That's one of the primary use cases. Hit record, reproduce the bug, narrate what you're seeing, and A-Eyes packages the whole thing and delivers it to your AI. Your assistant gets the full visual context instead of a text description.

Are my screen recordings sent to a server?

No. All captures are stored locally on your machine. There is no A-Eyes server, no telemetry, and no analytics. When you submit a capture, it goes directly to the AI agent you chose (Claude, Codex, etc.) through their own tools that you already use and trust. A-Eyes never sees your data.

Does transcription run locally?

Yes. A-Eyes uses a local Whisper model bundled with the app to transcribe your voice narration. Your audio never leaves your machine. The transcription is timestamped and synced with your screen frames so the AI can match what you said to what was happening on screen.

Does A-Eyes work on Windows and Mac?

Yes. A-Eyes runs natively on both Windows and macOS. It installs as a lightweight desktop app, lives in your system tray or menu bar, and launches with a global hotkey.
